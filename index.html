    About Me

    I am a data scientist with a strong background in physics and extensive experience in leveraging image processing, time series analysis, and AI for real-world applications. With one master’s degree in physics and another in energy and climate, along with research experience in computational astrophysics, I have worked on projects involving power line monitoring, extreme weather modeling, and object detection. My interests lie in generative AI, Bayesian inference, latent variable models, diffusion-based models, and the stochastic modeling of time series.

    Experience

    Super-resoultion

    A deep-learning-based super-resolution map of a weather front hitting the western coast of Norway on January 1, 2021. Low-res ERA-5 data with a resolution of \(30 \times 30 \, \text{km}^2\) was used as input. The super-resoultion map has a resolution of \(2.5 \times 2.5 \, \text{km}^2\). PyTorch was extensively used for the purpose of this project. The three points on the map show the location of three synoptic weather stations.

    Courtesy of [StormGeo]

    Synthetic Apperture Radar (SAR) Interferometry

    Subsidence and rise of Ground in Oslo (SAR interferometry map) for a period of one year (2019 - 2020) using Sentinel-1 data. The collected Sentinel-1 data were processed using SNAP software. The estimated points of subsidence and rise were extracted using geopandas and pyproj libraries. Finally, MapBox was used to over plot the extracted points on the map of the city of Oslo.

    Object Detection

    A proof of concept to estimate the parking space availability and traffic in an area in the city of Toronto, Canada, using deep-learning and high resolution satellite imagery. Each bounding box shows a detected vehicle. Scikit image library was extensively used for image preprocessing and breaking the satellite iamge into tiles for inference and later for stiching it back together.
